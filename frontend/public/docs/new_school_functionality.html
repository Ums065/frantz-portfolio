1. Challenge analysis

Program overview and stakeholders

The three client messages describe one system: a school-based challenge portal that turns community discovery into a structured project workflow. The site needs to capture people, approvals, evidence, submissions, and winners in one controlled flow.

Challenge mission

Students interview ten local businesses, identify the most important challenge they discover, and propose a practical solution that can help the business grow, improve visibility, increase sales, strengthen customer engagement, improve operations, or better serve the community.

  • Students must be between 14 and 19 years old.
  • Parent or guardian consent is required before participation is active.
  • Participants must register through FrantzCoutard.com.
  • The public message of the program is "Leave It Better Than You Found It".
  • The challenge is designed to build leadership, communication, and problem-solving skills.

Stakeholder map

Stakeholder Role in the system
Student Registers, gets a participant ID and QR code, logs ten businesses, and submits the project.
Parent / Guardian Creates an account from the QR flow and confirms legal consent, signature, and approval.
School Confirms the student attends the school and validates that the parent consent is real.
Teacher Monitors student progress, confirms participation, and tracks completion inside the school context.
Admin Reviews submissions, exports data, and selects winners.
Public Views the challenge page, rules, prize details, and winner announcement.

Business goal

Turn a school challenge into a measurable community impact workflow by capturing real business interviews, real approval evidence, and real project outcomes.

Website goal

Add a new school-focused module to the current site without breaking the existing brand language or dashboard patterns.

Data goal

Store every registration, approval, interview, and submission with timestamps, names, signatures, and status history.

2. Workflow

Workflow and gating logic

The system is a gated pipeline. Each stage unlocks the next one only when the previous stage is complete and stored with the correct approval or evidence record.

Workflow diagram one direction from registration to winner publishing

Critical status chain

1Student Registered
2Parent Consent Pending
3Parent Consent Approved
4School Approval Pending
5School Approval Approved
6Teacher Approval Pending
7Teacher Approval Approved
8Eligible To Submit
9Submission Complete

Gate rules

  • Students cannot submit until parent consent is approved.
  • Students cannot submit until school approval is approved.
  • Students cannot submit until teacher approval is approved.
  • Students cannot submit until ten completed business interviews are entered.
  • Every consent and approval record must store name, date, time, and signature.
  • Every student must have one unique QR code connected to their profile.
  • Parent information must be captured only through the student QR flow.
Rule: each stage unlocks the next only after the required record exists

Consent statements that must be accepted: I confirm I am the parent or legal guardian. I give permission for my child to participate. I understand my child will interview local businesses. I understand school verification is required.

Parent approval output

  • Student dashboard status changes to Parent Consent Approved.
  • Parent record becomes linked to the student record by QR token.

Why this flow matters

This avoids manual parent intake and forces the approval to happen from the student-specific QR code, which gives the system a clean relationship between student, parent, and consent audit trail.

5. Evidence capture

Student business interview module

This is the core evidence layer of the challenge. Each student must record ten local businesses, with enough context to explain what was discovered and what opportunity exists for improvement.

Interview prompts from the client

  • Does the business have a website?
  • Does the business have a Google Business Profile?
  • Does the business actively use social media?
  • Does the business use digital signage?
  • Does the business offer coupons or rewards programs?
  • Can customers order online?
  • Does the business have its own ordering platform?
  • Does the business rely solely on third-party delivery apps?
  • What is the biggest challenge facing the business?
  • What opportunities are being missed?

Business interview output

  • Each entry should save a complete interview snapshot with date and location context.
  • Business records should be editable until the student final submission is locked.
  • The system should count completed entries and show progress toward 10 / 10.
  • Student notes should capture both the pain point and the missed opportunity.

Unlock rule

The final submission step remains locked until all ten business interviews are completed and approved by the system as valid records.

Why this matters: the challenge is not just about writing a solution. It is about evidence-based discovery from ten real businesses in the student's neighborhood.
Rule: 10 completed business entries are required before final submission unlocks

Submission rules

  • The written submission must fit on one page.
  • The video must be no longer than 1 minute and 30 seconds.
  • Students cannot submit unless all three approvals are approved.
  • Students cannot submit unless ten business interviews exist.
  • Every submission should be tied to the student profile and participant ID.
  • Admin must be able to approve or reject submissions and leave notes.

Admin review actions

  • View student profile, parent consent, school approval, and teacher approval.
  • View all business entries submitted by the student.
  • Export data to CSV.
  • Select first, second, and third place winners.
  • Publish winners to FrantzCoutard.com.

9. Technical plan

How this fits the current site

The new_school module should be added to the current FrantzCoutard.com architecture instead of being built as a separate product. That keeps the brand, session model, and admin patterns consistent.

Recommended implementation approach

  • Add a new school module in the React frontend with pages for student registration, parent consent, school registration, teacher registration, dashboards, and admin review.
  • Extend the PHP API with new routes for registration, QR token validation, approvals, dashboard summaries, interview entries, submissions, exports, and winner publishing.
  • Add MySQL tables for students, parents, schools, teachers, approvals, business interviews, submissions, notifications, QR tokens, and winners.
  • Reuse the current site auth/session approach so the new module feels native to the existing portal.
  • Use the current upload pattern for files such as consent signatures, student videos, and written submissions.

Acceptance criteria

  • Student cannot submit without parent, school, and teacher approval.
  • Student cannot submit without ten completed business interviews.
  • Every approval stores name, date, time, and signature.
  • Parent information is captured only through the QR flow.
  • Admin can export data, choose winners, and publish the result page.
  • Teacher dashboard shows progress, rankings, and award status.
Phase 1 Build data model, QR token flow, and registration pages.
Phase 2 Add school and teacher dashboards, approvals, and business interview entry.
Phase 3 Add final submission upload, admin review tools, CSV exports, and rankings.
Phase 4 Publish winners on August 25, 2026 and connect the public challenge page.
Outcome: a native new_school module that matches the current site stack
